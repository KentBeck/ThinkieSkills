Thinkie Anatomy

Each Thinkie comes with a pattern & a transformation. I

  1. Match the pattern.

  2. Apply the transformation if the pattern matches.

  3. Evaluate the results.

Each step seems straightforward to me but the sequence seems hard. Other people seem to start with evaluating the expected results. If they don’t expect value, they give up.

Evaluating expected value a priori may be fine for highly predictable situations. I’m bored with predictable situations so I’m seldom in them for long. If you can’t guess value then you may as well try all the Thinkies all the time. One or two patterns might match. Then you evaluate the results of the transformation.

Can’t/Because

Pattern: Someone says, “We can’t X because of Y.”

Transformation: “When (not Y), then we can X.”

Example: We can’t release software after every change because there would be too many defects. → When we have fewer defects we can release software after every change.

At this point we can start to talk about ways & means. How would we have fewer defects? Quanitfy “fewer”. Is that reasonable/possible/economic? What should we do first?

This Thinkie is good for getting unstuck. When folks are making excuses, they don’t seem to notice that they are also telling themselves how to get unstuck. Your job isn’t to be smarter than they are, it’s just to notice.

Now?

Please try this Thinkie. Comment with stories of how it went.

Make a habit of converting Can’t/Because into When/Can. Tell a colleague that you’re trying it. Try it together. Keep notes of when you try it. My hypothesis is that soon it will become a habit. You won’t have to exert your will to apply it, the Thinkie will speak up when it applies. Then you can evaluate the results of the transformation.
