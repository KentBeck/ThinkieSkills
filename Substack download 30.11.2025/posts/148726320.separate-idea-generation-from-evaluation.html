  1. Try the idea (preferably a crazy one) on myself.

  2. Explain it 1x1 over & over until I find the totem.

  3. Repeat the message ad nauseam until I hear someone repeat the message to me.

A comment gave me hope that my message about generating ideas with Thinkies is coming through:

I am guessing that Kent will come back with his usual: “Thinkies are tools for generating ideas, not just good ideas” :-)

Thanks, . On the Idea to Impact path I’m somewhere on step 3.

My only quibble is with the word “just”. To maintain the flow of ideas I don’t want any consideration of quality while an idea is forming. I say, “Thinkies generate ideas, not good ideas.”

When a Thinkie generates an idea for you, it isn’t good or bad. It’s in Idea Limbo. That’s where it belongs. Let it form fully. Okay, I can see it now. The whole thing. I’m not committed to it. My ego won’t be bruised if it turns out to be useless. My self-image won’t be inflated if it turns out to be judged “brilliant” by others. It is its own thing, complete in itself.

Breathe in through the nose. Hold it. Out through the mouth. Okay, now how I can invalidate this idea as quickly & cheaply as possible?

This is the fundamental skill of Thinkies. However large or small your Thinkie collection, generating value depends on allowing ideas to form first. Taking a breath. And then evaluating the ideas cheaply.
