Yesterday I gave a new keynote at Beauty in Code & the response was polarized. Some folks sat there pretty clearly pissed off. Other folks came gushing up to me afterwards, thanking me for giving words to what they were experiencing at work.

The One Startling Sentence for the presentation is, “Forest & Desert can’t talk.” Words with seemingly-similar meanings have completely different emotional & intellectual impact.

The talk starts with a list of basic principles for each:

The point I was happiest about making is that the Forest assumes the innate goodness of (most) people while the Desert assumes people will get away with whatever they can get away with. Where you land on this question really shapes the rest of everything you do.

The second half of the preso was a list of words that have opposite meanings depending on where you are:

  • Metrics are tools for self-improvement (Forest) or a way to cut useless people (Desert).

  • Dependencies are areas of expertise shared with the community (Forest) or hilltop castles to be defended to the death (Desert).

  • Compliance is a source of ideas for improvement (Forest) or a bar to be hurdled with the least possible effort (Desert).

  • Accountability is an exercise in trust building (Forest) or a ritual for dispensing blame away from power (Desert).

  • Status updates are redundant (Forest) or a way to strip people away from functioning projects to rescue failing projects.

You can’t speak a sentence in the Forest & expect it to be understood in the Desert or vice versa. Two communities separated by a common language.
