Pattern: you’re faced with an evenly-balanced decision

Transformation: list the upside & downside consequences of the decision. Upside & no downside? Do it.

I know this one sounds trite, but stay with me. When I’m facing a decision, even something as trivial as “should I post this”, I can get so wrapped up in the details of the decision & the expectations about possibly implementing the decision that I forget to pay attention to the consequences.

Upside/downside is a great example of a Thinkie. I say, “What’s the upside & downside?”, I start thinking new thoughts, sometimes those thoughts help.

I hesitate to use Trump as an example because, well, downside, but there’s also upside because it’s a good example. What 2016 candidate Trump figured out was that the upside/downside balance in political communication had shifted. No longer were we in an era where one tank helmet picture could sink a campaign.

The Clinton campaign was still terrified of the downside of a bad message, so every communication went through layers & layers of cleansing before it became public. This slowed their responses & bleached all humanity from them.

Trump realized:

  • His supporters would never leave.

  • His detractors would never be won over.

  • More of the undecided would be attracted by whatever lying, crass, naive, thoughtless thing he said than would be repelled.

Upside & no downside.
