“Tidy first?” is a question that comes up ten times a day for programmers. You need to change the behavior of some code. The code is messy in a way that makes the change harder than necessary. Should you tidy it up before making the change?

Neither dogmatic answer satisfies. If you always tidy everything that’s messy, you’ll spend almost all your time tidying & little time delivering new features. If instead you focus on just changing the behavior of the code regardless of the mess, then the mess accumulates & changes become harder & harder. More than just the increasing cost of change are all the changes you don’t even try because you don’t want to face the mess.

The software design techniques discussed in Tidy First? will expand in subsequent books (also to be published here first) to techniques for teams of programmers & later to cross-functional teams. First, though, we’re going to focus on you & making your daily programming more effective.
