Pattern: People are working so as to sabotage progress towards a desired outcome.

Transformation: Look at their incentives from their perspective. Change the incentives. The behavior will follow. (Unless you screw up the incentives, which is easy to do.)

Sales people are poaching sales from other regions, resulting in lower overall sales for the company. Bad sales people!

Hang on. They are doing what they are incentivized to do. Make quota or else? I’ll make quota. So would you if you were in their position.

Identifying perverse incentives requires thinking, just for a moment, the worst of people. “What would I do if I was a greedy, venal, short-term, selfish person facing those incentives?” Then think about how to change the incentive system.

Note—by “incentive” I don’t mean “money”. This is a tiny part of a much larger conversation, but we are all incentivized by a broad range of factors. Some factors apply right now, some are from our past. Stories told about us incentivize. Stories told about us by us incentivize. Approval, status, recognition, belonging, sometimes disapproval—all incentivize us.
