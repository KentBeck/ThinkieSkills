Thank you for all the thoughtful comments & discussion about the first post on the topic. People are worried, afraid, maybe a little hopeful, but mostly resigned that the worst possible systemic effects are just what’s going to happen to programmers out of all this brouhaha.

My points from yesterday:

  • I’m not worried that the job of programmer will go away, because of various historical analogies. I could be wrong, but I’m happy going with this as an assumption.

  • AI is not being applied to helping programmers learn more faster.

  • AI is not being applied to improve code or system structure. Maybe we assume that an infinitely fast coding AI will be, well, infinitely fast so structure doesn’t matter. I don’t buy it.

Here are 2 more missing topics:

  • Feedback cycles

  • Human connection

Feedback Cycles

XP identifies the length & quality of feedback cycles as critical to software development. If you don’t know everything you’re going to know, & if what you learn is going to be important, then how fast you learn is more important than what you know right now.

This is related to the point above about learning, but specifically directed to the length of feedback cycles. I see tools that take minutes to run. Ouch.

If your goal was to shorten the latency between human decision & concrete feedback, how would you apply AI differently? Using this assumption you’re likely to come up with different tools than if the goal is to have AIs make bigger decisions & if that delays feedback oh well.

Human Connection

Another disturbing? regressive? trend I see in AI-based tools for software development is further isolating programmers. “See, you’ll be so fast that you’ll never have to talk to anybody ever again.”

XP is a set of rituals & rhythms for safe human interaction in the collective enterprise of developing software, even for people who don’t particularly care for human interaction.

Rather than assume isolation isn’t that bad & increasing isolation well oh well that won’t cause any problems, what if you applied AI to connecting programmers with each other, with users, with managers & other parts of the business?

My idea for CodeFeed is along these lines. What if you had a model watch all the changes to the system? It then introduced people with common goals, styles, or interests.

Conclusion

I’m sure there’ll be more. In fast moving times, it pays to move fast & worry about tidiness later.
