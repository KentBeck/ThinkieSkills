My Finances In Perspective

This is going to require some context. I’ve talked here about my finances, my four financial goals (predictable income exceeds predictable expenses, newsletter income exceeds predictable expenses, buy a house & accumulate toward retirement, never need to work again), and my progress towards those goals.

My basic financial challenge is that I have both liquidity & profitability problems. I have insufficient buffer to handle the variance in my revenue, leading to periodic liquidity squeezes. The lack of buffer leaves me vulnerable to declines in revenue. I also have a long-term profitability problem, with fixed & long-term liabilities colliding with declining late-career earning potential. This leads to considerable anxiety—how am I going to pay for July & also how am I going to pay for being 75?

In the first couple of months after publishing TF?, it seemed like book royalties would contribute significantly to my goals. Then royalties shrank, a combination of the end of one-time revenue like non-English licensing & returns from physical bookstores. Was book writing an economic activity for me or not?

I did some analysis to think about this question in context. First, what are my sources of recurring revenue? Royalties from my back catalog (Pearson), newsletter revenue (thank you!), royalites from TF? (O’Reilly), & my Mechanical Orchard salary.

(For scale, that first grid line above the data is about where my fixed expenses live.)

A couple of things stand out for me about this graph:

  • Back catalog royalties get paid every six months. Residual revenue isn’t everything I need but it sure is nice. Get you some residual income.

  • I was really losing ground before I joined Mechanical Orchard in November 2022.

  • Even with a stable salary my predictable revenue usually falls well short of my fixed expenses. I make up the difference with speaking & consulting.

  • That spike of TF? royalites + subscription revenue just before I raised prices was great! If every month was like that… Wishful thinking.

  • On this scale, my ongoing TF? royalties won’t contribute noticeably.
