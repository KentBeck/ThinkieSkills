Your software business can grow more quickly by growing more smoothly.

Every software business is simultaneously:

  • A stream of existing cash flows from existing features.

  • A portfolio of options for new features that create new cash flows.

Balancing these sources of value is part of the job of the software executive. But how?

  • The business side of the team wants more features sooner.

  • The technology side of the team wants to take time to do it right and, when the code has become a tangled knot of yarn, to do it over.

Again, balancing these voices, managing this healthy conflict, is part of the job of the software executive. But, again, how?

Tidy First is a style of software design that, with your help & leadership, reconciles these often-conflicting experiences of software development. Here’s how.

When software design happens in big chunks, whether at the beginning of a project or as a diving save after the project has grown out of control, you break the flow of new features. Tidy First operates on the principle of staged investment.

  • Do a good job of the things you know you need to do now.

  • Don’t speculate, wait. When software design is about to deliver value, that’s when you design.

  • When you get behind on design, you take your foot off the gas until your wheels stop spinning. Don’t stop, just steer towards more design investment for a while.

You’ll need to provide leadership to business & technology to maintain this dynamic balance. Their incentives are not naturally aligned, nor are they likely to trust each other. You’re there to ensure that you grind enough wheat to make enough bread to feed everyone but y’all also plant enough seeds for the next harvest.
