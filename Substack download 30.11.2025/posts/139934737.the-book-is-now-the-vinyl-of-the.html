I picked up a copy of Tidy First? the other day & thought, “This book is to thought what vinyl is to music.” What do I mean? How did I get here? What am I doing next?

Let’s gather a few threads.

Commodities & Leverage

The first is my quest for relevance in a world where many of my skills just got commodified. This led to my most-engaged tweet ever. (And a follow-up essay.)

The follow-up conversation centered around, “Okay, smart guy, what’s the 10%.” The thing about technological innovations is that rapid cost reductions uncover surprising second- and third-order consequences. You can’t analyze your way to figuring out those consequences from first principles—too many feedback loops confound closed-form analysis. You have to try stuff & see what sticks.

Fine Tuning

Once I got into explore mode, I was keen to try stuff. I have ChatGPT wired up to a button on my phone to reduce the friction to using it casually. I was therefore excited when OpenAI announced GPTs, the ability to fine-tune the base model.

I loaded a bunch of my content into a model I called Grumpy Old Programmer. The answer it gave were noticeably more like me than the generic model.

A former coaching student put some of Tidy First? into a similar model & yep, it could now tidy code.

Now I had a hint—fine-tuned models could in some sense represent my thoughts. Not perfectly, no, but have you seen what thoughtless folks did to TDD?

Text

I live an intellectual life expressed as text & as pictures.

TF? is my 10th book. Most books are around 50,000 words, so that’s 500K. For each word that appears in a book, I probably write 20 more in drafts, blogs, posts, comments, & emails. So call it 10M lifetime. If I type 50 words/minute, that’s 200K minutes writing, 1.2 working years. So 3% of my working life has been spent putting words in a row.

These words & pictures go into a variety of formats.

The items above are the intended outputs of writing. For 40 years I have written notes which lead to conversations which lead to posts & comments which lead to videos, then talks, & finally books.

New World, Dimly Seen

The old picture is that I have thoughts which I render as text & pictures which is then viewed by a bunch of people. (Before you ask, I have no idea why my left ear is so much longer. Maybe I have a heavy earring, I dunno.)

I was holding up a copy of my book while discussing fine-tuned models when a new picture started to emerge.

Musicians used to record albums. Now they publish songs which are streamed, remixed, turned into videos, used as background for other videos. Sometimes, a bunch of songs go together well & get turned into an album. Really special albums get turned into vinyl.

The thing that used to drive the process is now an optional status symbol towards the end of the process, merchandise almost (except you can play it for a particular listening experience).

Intellectual life—the life of ideas, of figuring out ideas that work, then figuring out how to explain them, then figuring out their limits, then figuring out why they work—could become me feeding my personal model. People wanting to take in what I’m thinking about can do so, but in a form of their choosing. Some will read text, some will watch videos, some will read a physical book.

Writing a book will still be a thing. For Tidy First? I took 18 years of thinking about software design—words, conversations, pictures, talks—& distilled it into a sequence that I think will help lead some folks through their learning. I learned a ton in the process. I learned what I actually understood & what I didn’t. I learned what I could explain & what I couldn’t. I learned how theory connected to practice & practiced connected to theory.

Even if I have a little intellectual angel sitting on my shoulder, I still want all the benefits of writing books. I want to look back over a body of work & make sense of it as a whole.

Some folks, though, will be on a more real-time journey with me & I with them. Their model will query my model & render my thoughts in a form they can best use.

Exploration

As is my way, I’m exploring this new model in various cheap ways:

  • Fine tuning various models with various content of mine to see how the answers differ from the generic model’s answers.

  • Using Siri & Notes to record more fine-grained thoughts, then feeding those thoughts into models & how that changes either my thinking or the models’ answers.

  • A larger, longer project on creativity called Thinkies that is consciously using this style of thoughts fed into a larger model (currently manual but soon to be represented as a real language model).

  • Thinking about the followup to Tidy First? as “thoughts fed into a model & then rendered” (currently prototyped as index cards—again, to be fed into a real model).
