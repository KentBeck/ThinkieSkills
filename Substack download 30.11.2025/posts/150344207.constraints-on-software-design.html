I’m liking my new talk about Empirical Software Design. Here’s a short preview.

I was giving a talk at GOTO CPH & Dave Farley preceded me. He gave a clear, commandments-oriented presentation about when to do design. I thought okay so I don’t have to do that, but I can add some “why”. Here’s what I came up with.

There are 3 sets of constraints on design that should shape any strategy (Empirical Design is only one) for the timing & magnitude of design investment.

People

  • The design strategy needs to acknowledge that human relationships are the center of design. If you get the code right & the people wrong, you damage the effects.

  • Aligning authority & responsibility is a key to healthy relationships. Organizations often encourage misaligned authority & responsibility, so we’re paddling a bit upstream on this one.

  • People & teams change, so the strategy needs to be prepared to accommodate & encourage positive change.

Economics

  • Survival is the first goal. A design strategy that encourages a higher short-term survival rate dominates. You can’t bank long-term advantages if there’s no longer term.

  • Net present value. Spending later is the same as spending less, earning sooner is the same as earning more. The design strategy needs to acknowledge this.

  • Optionality. Design creates options for future change. Those options have real economic value & should be treated as investment opportunities.

Software

Software is a unique medium with unusual properties. The design strategy needs to adapt.

  • Constantine’s Equivalence—the cost of the software is approximately equal to the cost of changing the software which is approximately equal to the cost of the large changes. Manage those large changes & you have leverage over costs.

  • Coupling transmits & amplifies cost. Sometimes you can eliminate coupling. Sometimes you can reduce its cost by reducing coupling’s scope (increasing cohesion).

Conclusion

Empirical Software Design is my response to these constraints. I’d much rather have conversations about the constraints than arguments about details.
