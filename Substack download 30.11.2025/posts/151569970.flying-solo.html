I will announce later today that I am stepping down as Chief Scientist of Mechanical Orchard. I believe in the mission of MO. I believe in the people there. What it came down to is that I have a limited number of days left on the planet (I don’t know how many—this isn’t any kind of medical announcement) & I want to spend as many of them as possible choosing what I want to do.

The good news is that my content business is doing well enough that I can reasonably take this step.

Today

Here’s a snapshot of my year-to-date revenue & expenses (excluding my (now former) salary). I’m almost there. In fact, I would have been there but I recently started paying full price for my health insurance. Ouch.

Because I was over replacement during the year, I accumulated enough cushion to believe that I can increase revenue to make up the shortfall before it becomes a problem. In recent language, I’m going to act like I’m in the forest, not the desert.

Plan

So, where’s the new revenue coming from?

Substack

Subscriber revenue has flattened out after the price increase. Flat subscriber revenue is consistent with the experience of other newsletter publishers.

More revenue will come from sponsored posts. I’ve talked to folks who make twice the revenue from sponsored posts that they do from paying subscribers. I want to be keenly aware when I’m sending out something that is not what paying subscribers are paying for, even though it’s going to all subscribers. Your goodwill is what makes this move even possible.

The potential of sponsored posts is doubling my newsletter revenue, but that assumes I market & sell well. Not great assumptions, historically, but we’ll see how I learn. OTOH, if your company wants to be the long-term sponsor—Tidy First Sponsored By XYZ—hit me up.

Speaking

I was surprised at how large a portion of my revenue came from speaking gigs. Conference speaking seldom pays well. The revenue comes from bespoke talks given private to a single company. Remote talks are (seemingly) reasonably priced—nobody complains. If I have to travel I charge 3-4X. Then I don’t mind traveling & I don’t complain.

More speaking revenue depends on speaking more frequently, doing a better job of marketing & selling. I also need to hit the conference circuit. Video clips from those talks seem like they should be great marketing material.

Consulting

I rarely get called for consulting, which is a pity because it pays well, I find the work stimulating & interesting, & I get good-to-excellent reports afterwards. Again with the marketing & selling.

Royalties

You don’t write technical book to make money, but Tidy First? has been doing consistently well since last year’s release. It’s too soon to know what The Good News Factory will contribute. If Tidy Together does anything like TF? then royalties will grow a bit.

Thinkies are another wild card. When will I turn them into a book? How will that book do? (Watch this space for an exciting (to me) Thinkies-related announcement in the coming weeks.)

Dividends

Some of my tech investments have turned into profitable companies that are returning a bit of cash. No plans to grow, but nice to have.

Workshops/Training

I don’t currently offer any workshops, but it seems like this could be a revenue source on the scale of speaking. I’d especially like it if I didn’t have to personally give the workshops once they’d been launched & refined.

Employment

Just because I’m not employed at the moment doesn’t mean I’m off the market. If there’s someone out there who wants a Distinguished Engineer who can level up Staff+ engineers, encourage positive culture growth, & contribute to strategic decisions, I’m interested. It’d be expensive but worth it.

Long Term

I’ll be frank. I don’t like the long-term looks of this situation. I lost my assets in my 2016 divorce & incurred a life-long alimony liability. I have been able to bounce back reasonably well given those constraints, but I don’t have lots to fall back on should I wish to stop working & retire. Basically as things stand now I can never afford to retire. Good thing I like working.

There is no amount of money that provides financial security, not with the state of the US healthcare industry. However, I wish I was making enough to accumulate a larger cushion. We’ll see in a year whether my plan above results in:

  • Down 20% in a down market

  • Getting comfortably above fixed costs

  • Taking off for some reason.

The ways to get to secure-r retirement savings are:

  • Win one of the equity lottery tickets or

  • Earn enough to accumulate &

  • Preserve the ability to continue to earn. To do this I’m swimming upstream of tech’s youth bias.
