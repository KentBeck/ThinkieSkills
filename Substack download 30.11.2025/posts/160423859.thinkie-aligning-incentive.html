Pattern: You can see that folks are working at cross purposes

Transformation: Give them an incentive that will influence their actions towards a common goal

When I say “incentive”, folks immediately think “money”. While money is a powerful incentive, it also tends to distort behavior, precisely because it is so useful & so personal. Still, “$100 to everyone when we all get there,” will influence behavior.

More powerful still is a sense of belonging. Take Goldratt’s Scout story—tying a group of hikers together gives them an aligning incentive. Everyone gets there or no one does.

Contrast Thinkie Aligning Incentive with pure talk with no backup. “You guys really need to work together!” Nothing is going to change. They aren’t working together for reasons, & you need to change the reasons or the behavior won’t change.
