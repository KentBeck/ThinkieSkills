September 1, 2028

Colleagues,

What I’m most proud of during my time at Roblox is how I lived a great life the whole time while having significant impact. My entire career I’ve never managed to maintain those two goals for any length of time. Here I did it for 4 years. No migraines. No blood pressure problems. I stayed fit, creative, I travelled. I built & maintained my relationships, in and out of tech.

At Roblox I tackled new challenges with rests between. That was the key, a rhythm of finding an opportunity, addressing it, passing the torch, then sitting (actively) until I spotted the next opportunity. Engineers have been promoted, teams have been righted, engineers now come to Roblox because of the outstanding opportunities it offers talented, potential-filled young-uns.

I didn’t have this impact by blindly copying past successes. I started teams. I managed teams (briefly). I initiated product features. I contributed to boardroom decisions. And yes I coached & wrote & spoke & counseled & mentored.

To bring this reminiscence full circle, living a good life & contributing to Roblox paid me back. I did my part to boost the stock price & now I have financial security & options I didn’t have 4 years ago. My time at Roblox built me a house, something I’ve dreamed of since I was a kid but had kind of given up hope for.

I only planned to be here 3 years but I’ve been having so much fun I made it 4. Now, though, it’s time to move on. Time for art, for travel, for stories, for poker, all while I still can.

I’m not really retiring—you won’t get rid of me that easily. I won’t say goodbye. I’ll say auf wiedersehen. See you further down the road.

Kent Beck
