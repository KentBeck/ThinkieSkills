Pattern: You’re stalled trying to make progress changing the outcome of a tradeoff.

Transformation: Rather than trying to change the outcome directly, change the shape of the tradeoff curves.

Continuous deployment is an example. We’d like to deploy more frequently for lots of good reasons. However, the cost per deployment is killing us.

I’ve seen folks just decree daily/weekly/monthly deployments in situations like this. Chaos ensues. Errors go up. Overhead goes up. Work done goes down. Nobody is happy.

Instead, think about deployment frequency as a tradeoff:

On one side we have the cost of deploying more slowly—cost of delaying feedback, cost of delaying revenue, cost of errors from deploying too many changes at once, the cost of the human energy lost when we don’t see the effects of our efforts.

On the other side we have the cost of deployment overhead, the cost of errors from insufficient testing.

Framing deployment frequency as the outcome of a tradeoff lets us predict what will happen if we just start deploying more frequently—costs will rise.

Changing the Curves

We aren’t helpless victims, we are participants in a system. What if we invest in lowering the overhead of deployment?

Now deploying more frequently lowers costs.

If we want to increase deployment frequency further, we know one thing we can do—look at the tradeoff curves anew & see what we can do to change them.

Thinkie Tradeoff Curves transforms a situation in which we have to give up something we want in order to get something else we want into a situation where we invest in changing the curves & then we get more of what we want on every dimension. (This trick doesn’t always work. Say it with me—Thinkies generate ideas, not good ideas.)
