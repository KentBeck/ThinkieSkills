What follows includes frank discussion of my personal finances as a nearing-retirement person under difficult constraints in 2024 America. If such discussion bothers you, scroll, as the kids say these days, on.

Tidy First? has done very well for a technical book. I’ve gotten great feedback from people who found value in its discussion of optionality & cohesion & structure/behavior. Yes, there were some complaints about how short the book was, but we’ve discussed my plan here from time to time so you know that was both expected & I don’t take it personally.

With all that, I was stalling on getting started writing book 2. And I wasn’t sure why I wasn’t writing. I knew what I wanted to say. It took a long walk on Montara Beach to help me come to grips with what was holding me back.

Summary: money.

Tidy Together?

Here’s the thing—I stress about money every day. I check my Stripe dashboard multiple times a day, my bank balances at least daily.

I start each month behind. How am I going to make up the predictable difference? Some months I catch up, once in a while I more than catch up (again, thank you!), sometimes I fall behind. The times I put money out of my mind for a few months, those months I definitely fall behind.

Because most of my expenses are fixed, I really can’t afford to fall behind. If I ever do, I’ll basically never catch up.

Now, my worst case scenario isn’t all that bad. I’ll always have a bed, food, shelter. But I’d rather stay adulting independently as long as I can manage it.

What does all this have to do with writing Tidy Together? Those 2 good months of O’Reilly revenue had me hoping that book royalties would contribute to financial security. Then the revenue slowed & I got discouraged. I guess writing wasn’t going to help me not have to worry after all.

The long walk on the beach was the moment I surrendered to this reality. Why else was I writing?

  • To really learn & understand the topic of software design.

  • As a service to geeks.

  • To find & build community around superior software design & see how much further we could push it.

That was it. Were those reasons good enough for me to write? Oh hell yes. And so I started writing (as you’ve seen). I was trying to use money to motivate myself to write. A part of me knew that the money wasn’t there. Predictable outcome—no writing. I accepted that I’m still contributing to causes I care about—writing.

Conclusion

I’m not asking for pity. My situation is the result of my choices & actions. I’ve made & lost 2 small fortunes, one to taxes & the other to divorce. I publish here because I’m trying to understand myself & sometimes geeks find my self-examination helpful. I’m not the only one facing a similar situation.

I live a good life. I’m frugal but I don’t deprive myself. It wouldn’t help. I have 6 months of cushion in the bank & another year’s worth in my 401K. My Gusto stock, should it ever become liquid, will give me another ~2 year’s worth of cushion. It’s not a whole retirement. Barring a big exit, that’s out of reach. Even owning a house is out of reach. That’s my situation.

In 30 years none of this will matter a single fart. In the meantime I have a book to write. I can’t wait.
