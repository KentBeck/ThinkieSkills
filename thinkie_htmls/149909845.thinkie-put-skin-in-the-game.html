Thinkie alert! This is a great example of me using a Thinkie. I read Alex’ heartbreaking message, matched the pattern, & suggested the idea that the Thinkie generated.

Pattern: Someone needs to make better decisions. They have plenty of information. They just aren’t using it well.

Transformation: Re-arrange feedback so the decision maker directly experiences the consequences of their decisions.

World War II aircraft maintenance chiefs would fly along on the first ride after major repairs. The Code of Hammurabi dictates that a builder who builds a house that collapses & kills the owner will themselves be killed.

Putting skin in the game may seem like harsh justice but the net effect is to reduce harm. The mechanic who will die if their work puts the pilot at risk will not work in a way that will put the pilot at risk.

Extending the example above, the device designer should be wearing an insulin pump, using today’s version of the software. “But these ‘requirements’ come from the FDA.” Okay, then the FDA auditor should also be wearing a pump. If they are already accepting responsibility for millions of lives, it’s a small additional sacrifice.

As with all Thinkies, the generated idea needs to be evaluated. It would be a big change for product owners, I’m sure, a big investment. But compared to the potential benefit?
