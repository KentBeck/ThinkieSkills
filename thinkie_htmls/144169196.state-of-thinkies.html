My goal with the Thinkies project is to encourage creative thinking. As my ideas go, it’s an infant. I wish I had dated my first index card, but I’m guessing the project (re-) started in 2019. (I had an earlier version in the 90s called “How I Think”—yeah, I was an arrogant jackass.)

My projects tend to only become mainstream (the ones that become mainstream) after 20 years, so Thinkies are a long ways from that. Still, I appreciate your attention every day. Your encouragement, attention, examples, & yes money keep me engaged.

So, where are we & where are we going with Thinkies?

Hypotheses

Thinkies rest on a string of somewhat-dubious hypotheses:

  1. Transformations can generate ideas that are, cumulatively, valuable.

  2. These idea generators can be collected & taught.

  3. People can learn to apply these idea generators out in the real world, with all its distractions.

  4. People can learn a large enough portfolio of Thinkies to consistently out-think those who don’t.

How’re we doing?

  1. Yes. Wasn’t really a question but the Thinkie framing is a little different. The body of Thinkies above cover a lot of situations. (Interesting side question: are there domain-specific Thinkies?)

  2. Yes, mostly. The comments I get show people remember the Thinkies & apply them. Not all Thinkies get those comments, though, so there’s obviously a ways to go in a) communicating & b) maybe culling/prioritizing the portfolio.

  3. Yes, the comments show that folks apply Thinkies to their actual situation & appreciate (some of) the ideas generated.

  4. Unknown. 70-80 Thinkies seems like a lot to me. What is a workable “vocabulary”? What is the best way to teach Thinkies? How do Thinkies compare to other approaches?

Next?

  • Drafts. Draft all the Thinkies. Should take 6 months or so.

  • Workshop. The high-feedback option is to hold workshops. Format? Location? On-line? Audience?

  • Book. Collect them into a book. That’s going to be a lot of work, especially collecting examples (which is why I appreciate all the examples in the comments).
