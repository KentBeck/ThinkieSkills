Pattern: You have a question, any question, especially a question you have tended to abandon because it would be too much work.

Transformation: Ask the genie.

This is a habit. A new habit. But a habit. A highly profitable but also highly variable habit. That is, sometimes the genie has a great answer & sometimes just distraction, but the great answers are really great & not something you would come up with another way.

Josh Cannon taught me this. Every time I would ask him a question he’d say, “Ask Claude!” Eventually that voice started playing in my head without me having to wait for him.

My most profound experience of this came when mentioned eigenvalues. I sighed & shrugged my shoulders, having avoided for decades digging that far into matrix math. Then I thought, “Wait a minute! Genie, teach me about eigenvalues.”

We had to go back & forth a bit. I pushed back on the examples that didn’t make sense & indeed the genie was wrong sometimes & sometimes I just didn’t understand yet. A half hour later I had a bit of an intuition about eigenvalues & their relationship to oscillation. Not enough to teach you, but enough for me for now. And that chat is still sitting there if I want to dig deeper.

So, every time you ask yourself, “I wonder…”, try to quickly figure out a way to ask the genie. Comment here with surprising applications of Thinkie Ask the Genie.
