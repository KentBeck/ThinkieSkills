Pattern: Is this small thing competition for this large thing?

Transformation: Compare growth rates, not absolute size. If the small thing is growing much faster then yes, take it seriously.

Early in COVID I used Thinkie Exponential Growth. People were saying, “Why all the shut downs? They’re saying 50,000 people might die. 50,000 people die in traffic accidents every year. We don’t shut down the roads.”

False equivalence. There’s no path from 50K traffic deaths to 500K. If traffic deaths are up or down a few percent there’s strong reaction. Apply Thinkie Exponential Growth. COVID deaths are still growing. The rate of infection is still growing. So COVID isn’t at all like traffic deaths & deserves a different response.

Or right now the migration to BlueSky. “Yeah, but BlueSky is 18 millionMAUs & Twitter is 400 million. 1/20th? Feh.” Thinkie Exponential Growth says look at the growth rates. Twitter is shrinking. BlueSky is growing fast. You can plot the cross-over point. (Ask your friendly local LLM or do the math yourself.)

Does BlueSky compete with Twitter despite the size difference? Yes.
