Pattern: You’re stuck solving a big problem.

Transformation: Imagine a sequence of steps that seem like they would solve the problem.

Scale Down unstucked the problem solving process by ignoring the whole problem & just addressing a seemingly-knowable seeming-subpart. Composed Solution, by contrast, solves the whole problem by seeing it as a pipeline. You don’t need perfect knowledge of any given step, you just need confidence that if we could do these steps in a row, the whole problem would be solved.

A key part of Composed Solution is imagining the intermediate results. What form will they take? For example, compilers use various intermediate forms to represent a partially compiled program.
