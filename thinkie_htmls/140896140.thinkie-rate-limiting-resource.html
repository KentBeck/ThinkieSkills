Trigger: Your product is growing fast but you’re nervous about the limits of that growth.

Transformation: What is the one resource you’re going to run out of next? How can you increase the supply & decrease the demand to delay the day of reckoning?

This Thinkie applies to the Expand portion of the growth story. You’ve found an itchy spot with your product. People are taking to it faster than you service. They are complaining that it’s too slow, too unreliable, it’s missing critical features. Congratulations!

During the Expand phase you’ll be tempted to try to solve all these problems at once. Don’t. Your organization is too new to be able to juggle priorities. Also, if you provide insufficient effort to one of those problems, your product is dead & you don’t get to keep playing.

Identify your rate-limiting resource. Plot the growth of supply & demand. Where demand exceeds supply is the day your product dies.

During Expand, it’s your job to 1) survive & 2) preserve as much potential growth as possible. But 1) survive. Bend the supply curve up. Bend the demand curve down.

It’s okay if you can still see when your product dies, as long as that date is off the current calendar.

Now find the next rate-limiting resource.

This Thinkie came up while advising an AI startup. They had a potential hit, growing fast, sticky product, but they couldn’t bring GPUs online fast enough to address their rapidly growing waitlist. They had lots of ideas for new product features, new ways to market & sell. Nope, their only job was to increase the supply of GPUs & to reduce the demand for GPUs created for each new customer.
