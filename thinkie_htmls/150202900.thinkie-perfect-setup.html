Pattern: you’re having trouble making progress in a situation.

Transformation: list the properties that would set the situation up perfectly for success. Back off only reluctantly.

Too often the reason we’re not making progress isn’t our effort, but our context. We get used to the proverbial pebble in our shoe & complain about our walking speed instead of taking the damn pebble out.

Poker is a great example of this for me. The perfect setup for a profitable poker session is:

  • Good sleep the night before.

  • Meditation.

  • Exercise.

  • Eating well.

  • Feeling sharp & confident.

  • Have a learning goal.

  • Pick the most profitable game.

  • Pick the table with the weakest opponents.

  • Have 2 conservative players taking their turns right after mine.

  • Have 1-2 players who don’t care about money/are drunk/are easy to read taking their turns right before mine.

If I have a session set up like this, it’s bound to be advantageous. (I can still lose short-term because hey poker.)

What of these properties are optional? If I want to play profitably, I should insist to myself on all the properties that are under my control. Do I? No, but more & more over time.

Extreme Programming is an exercise in Thinkie Perfect Setup. Strong social ties. Rapid feedback at all scales. Constant adjustment to evolving reality.

People’s reaction to XP often amazed me. “Well, of course if we had a customer on the team we’d succeed.” Um, okay?

I’m not so interested in how we can eek out some progress in a nearly-impossible situation. How can we set the situation up for success & keep it set up for success?
