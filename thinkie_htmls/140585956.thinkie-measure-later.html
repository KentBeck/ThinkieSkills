We continue to edge into systems thinking territory. Humans tend to look at what is easy to look at instead of what is important or that provides leverage. This Thinkie addresses that tendency.

Pattern: you’re stuck trying to improve a multi-step process

Transformation: what is something you could measure later in the process?

The classic example of this was managers concerned about programming productivity. If only the programmers typed more, we’d get more value out. Except that it didn’t matter how much code they produced, because QA could only validate so many changes.

This proved frustrating for programmers, because it acknowledged that they had no control over value delivered. It was also frustrating because it implied that some programmers should be testing & testing was low-status work. Boo hoo.

A variant of this Thinkie is to measure not just later, but bigger. Instead of “features/programmer/day” look at revenue or profit. Those larger measures will be vaguer but will offer more freedom of action. Larger measures also tend to share well.

Just to emphasize the contradictory nature of Thinkies, there will also be one for measuring smaller/sooner. Generate ideas, not good ideas.
