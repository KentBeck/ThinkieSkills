Pattern: Two or more parties are stuck in a dysfunctional relationship but everyone refuses to change.

Transformation: Take a step back & show the whole dynamic to all parties.

These kinds of change-encouraging Thinkies are fraught. People don’t make rational choices. I still tend to assume they do, leaving me baffled most of the time. However, it’s just not in me to do nothing.

The dynamic here is that everyone thinks they will lose if they change so they stay still no matter how unpleasant the status quo.

One of my most vivid professional encounters with a Nash Equilibrium was when interviewing a team that was perpetually late delivering. I asked the team how they estimated. They walked me through their process, including adding padding to individual tasks & then to the schedule as a whole.

“Why all this padding? You don’t think it will actually take this long.”

“Yes, but management always cuts our estimates in half.”

(Ironic because those “padded” estimates were accurate historically.)

Then I went to talk to the executives.

“We have to cut their estimates in half because we know they are sandbagging.”

“But they are always late.”

I was confused. I got everyone in a room & pled with them to just be straight with each other. They ignored me. But I tried.

Both parties felt like if they were honest about their estimates they would definitely lose. Neither could change. And so the wasteful (to me—to them it was safe) dance continued.
