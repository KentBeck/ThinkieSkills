Pattern: You need to act in a context where you can’t predict what the result of actions will be.

Transformation: Treat the choice of action as moves around a search space. Characterize the space.

  1. Ensure that actions are reversible.

  2. Identify the dimensions of variability.

  3. Observe whether the space is smooth or spiky. Do small changes in action result in large changes in outcome?

  4. Identify the cost of missing a good outcome.

Then search:

  1. Take an action, at random if you have no better starting place.

  2. Use your observation about smooth versus spiky to determine how far away to take the next action.

  3. Be prepared to update the dimensions of variability based on experience.

  4. Optimize to reduce the cost of experiments.

  5. Stop an experiment as soon as you have your answer.

For example, take the search for police evidence. Small differences in where you search make a big difference in whether you find a clue. Your search should be thorough & methodical.

Product decisions in a rich & rapidly emerging sector are spiky but there are probably many sources of value. Mostly take big jumps with the occasional small step.

Exploring a search space is a tricky mix of intuition & chaos. Your intuition will probably be speaking loudly, because it wants predictable success. If it’s truly a search, though, you can’t predict success, you can only discover it.
