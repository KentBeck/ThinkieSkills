Pattern: Two or more parties are struggling to cooperate.

Transformation: Model the situation as a Prisoners’ Dilemma. If it fits, apply Thinkie Put Skin In The Game or iteration to break the impasse.

To recap, Prisoners’ Dilemma is a game where 2 parties are arrested. If neither confesses, they both go free. If one confesses & the other doesn’t, the squealer goes free & the other prisoner goes to prison. If they both confess, they both go to prison but for less time.

See if the parties in the impasse are feeling like they are in a Prisoners’ Dilemma. “If I work on this urgent task Saturday & you don’t work Saturday I’ll feel like a sucker.”

What makes the Prisoners’ Dilemma intractable is the lack of information. The way out? Information.

  • Thinkie Put Skin In The Game signals that one party is opening themself up to negative consequences.

  • Iteration. If the same 2 prisoners play Prisoners’ Dilemma 100 times, they have incentive to build a habit of cooperation (not confessing). Slice your cooperation into many iterations & see how that works.

  • Call out the dilemma. Sometimes it helps to just say, “You’re afraid of being taken advantage of. Me too. <nervous laughter> What are we going to do about this?”
