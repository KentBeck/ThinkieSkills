Transformation: Treat the effect as the cause.

Let’s say as your organization has grown there are so many meetings that no one can get any work pushed to production any more. You try just eliminating meetings but they spring back up like mushrooms after rain. It’s normal to see this as meetings causing slowness. Meeting → slowness.

What if, instead of meetings causing slowness, slowness is causing meetings? You used to make tens, then hundreds of changes a day to your system. Now you want to make thousands. But your organization just isn’t capable of making thousands of changes a day without fatally destabilizing your system.

Thinkie Reverse Causality suggests you consider the meetings as an adaptive response, preventing an excess of changes from reaching production. Slowness → meetings. The solution isn’t eliminating meetings, it’s reducing the disruption of changes. Once you can handle thousands of changes, the meetings will evaporate like summer morning dew. [ed: see also Thinkie Match the Pipes.]
