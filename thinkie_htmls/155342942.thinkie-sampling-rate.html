Pattern: You are monitoring a system & feel like you’re missing something.

Transformation: Increase your sampling rate.

Observing a system is a tradeoff between the cost of observation, the accuracy of observation. If you feel like you’re missing something, think about the ratio of the duration of events of interest to the frequency of sampling.

You’re sampling at some rate:

If events are short compared to this rate, you’re likely to miss them:

Sample more often:

However, this is a tradeoff. Each sample consumes resources. Reduce the cost per sample or reduce the sampling rate until you get a clear picture at a reasonable cost.

I had an example of this recently where I was anxious about finances so I was checking my bank account balance multiple times a day. Each sample gave me some reassurance but it also set up more anxiety in the future. My bank account balance changes meaningfully maybe twice a month. My sampling rate was too high.
